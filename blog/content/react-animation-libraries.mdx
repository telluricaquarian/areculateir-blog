## What Are Cult Metrics?

Cult metrics aren’t in Ads Manager dashboards. They live in the cultural bloodstream around your brand. Signs include:  

- **Aesthetic Resonance** — when people copy your design language in decks, ads, or even competitor sites.  
- **Mimicry in the Wild** — when your phrasing, memes, or visuals start showing up uncredited in your niche.  
- **Memetic Velocity** — how fast your assets (screenshots, taglines, packaging shots) circulate without you paying for it.  
- **Signal Density** — the frequency of unsolicited references to your brand in communities you don’t directly control.  

These aren’t “soft” signals. They’re predictive of *compound growth* before the numbers hit your Stripe dashboard.

---

## Why They Matter

Relying only on CAC and ROAS blinds you to cultural drift. Paid media tells you what you bought, not what you seeded. Cult metrics flip the frame: they measure what spreads when you stop spending.  

Agencies and founders who track them can anticipate breakout moments, long before the analytics look sexy.

## Practical Ways to Measure

1. **Social Graph Tracking** — build lightweight scrapers for how often your assets get reshared.  
2. **Creative Copycats** — set alerts for competitors adopting your style.  
3. **Keyword Drift** — track unique words you coined (product names, taglines) appearing in new contexts.  
4. **Community Listening** — mine mentions in private groups, not just public feeds.  

---

## The Founder’s Lens

Cult brands aren’t accidental. They’re engineered through signals, aesthetics, and narrative. If you want exponential reach, you need to ask:  

- *What’s being copied?*  
- *Where are we being referenced without attribution?*  
- *What cultural artifacts are traveling faster than our paid CPMs?*  

That’s how you measure inevitability.

## Conclusion

Financial metrics tell you what happened. Cult metrics tell you what’s about to happen. The founders who learn to track resonance, mimicry, and spread aren’t just running businesses — they’re building inevitabilities.
